Mark this cycle as atypical

Sometimes a cycle behaves unusually for reasons unrelated to your normal fertility pattern. Marking it atypical excludes it from your cycle length averages so it doesn't throw off your predictions.

Common reasons a cycle may be atypical:

  • Illness, fever, or infection during the cycle
  • Significant stress or emotional trauma
  • Travel across time zones or major schedule disruption
  • Starting, stopping, or changing hormonal medication
  • Extreme changes in exercise or diet (e.g. rapid weight loss)
  • Postpartum cycles or cycles after a pregnancy loss
  • Very early or very late period with no clear cause

If unusual cycles happen frequently without a known reason, it may be worth mentioning to a doctor.
