OPKs (Ovulation Predictor Kits) are home urine tests — similar in format to a pregnancy test stick — that detect the surge in luteinizing hormone (LH) that your body releases 24–36 hours before ovulation. When the test line is as dark or darker than the control line, you're likely to ovulate within the next day or two. They're widely available at pharmacies (Clear Blue, First Response, etc.), inexpensive, and considered one of the most reliable ways to pinpoint your actual ovulation day rather than estimating it from calendar math.

They're definitely worth including — especially since many people trying to conceive use them, and understanding when to test and how to read the result is not always obvious. I'd suggest putting them in the Guide tab under Ovulation.
